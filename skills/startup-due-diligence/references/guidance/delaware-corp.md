# Delaware C-Corporation Guide

## Why Delaware?

Most VC-backed startups incorporate in Delaware because:
- Well-developed corporate law
- Specialized Court of Chancery
- Predictable legal outcomes
- Standard for institutional investors
- Flexible corporate statute

## Formation Requirements

### Certificate of Incorporation

**Required contents:**
- Company name (must include Corp, Inc, etc.)
- Registered agent name and address
- Purpose (typically "any lawful activity")
- Authorized stock (classes, shares, par value)
- Incorporator name and address

**Common provisions:**
- Board authority for stock issuance
- Elimination of director liability (102(b)(7))
- Exclusive forum for disputes
- Prohibition on cumulative voting

### Bylaws

**Typical contents:**
- Meeting procedures (board and stockholders)
- Notice requirements
- Quorum requirements
- Officer positions and duties
- Indemnification provisions
- Amendment procedures

### Initial Actions

**Board organizational consent should include:**
- Adoption of bylaws
- Election of officers
- Approval of stock issuances
- Authorization of bank accounts
- Adoption of equity incentive plan
- Approval of form agreements
- Ratification of pre-incorporation actions

## Stock Structure

### Common Stock

**Typical rights:**
- Voting (one share = one vote)
- Dividends (when declared)
- Liquidation (after preferred)

**For startups:**
- Founders receive common stock
- Option pool is common stock

### Preferred Stock (Post-Priced Round)

**Typical rights:**
- Liquidation preference
- Anti-dilution protection
- Protective provisions
- Board seats
- Information rights
- Pro rata rights

**Created via:**
- Certificate of Designation or
- Amended and Restated Certificate

### Authorized vs. Issued Shares

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| Authorized | Maximum shares company can issue |
| Issued | Shares actually issued to holders |
| Outstanding | Issued shares still held (not repurchased) |
| Reserved | Allocated for specific purposes (options) |
| Available | Authorized - Issued - Reserved |

## Corporate Actions

### Board Actions

**Actions typically requiring board approval:**
- Stock issuances
- Option grants
- Officer appointments
- Material contracts
- Compensation decisions
- Strategic decisions

**Methods:**
- Board meeting with minutes
- Written consent (requires all directors in DE)

### Stockholder Actions

**Actions typically requiring stockholder approval:**
- Director elections
- Charter amendments
- Major transactions (merger, sale)
- Equity plan adoption

**Methods:**
- Annual or special meeting
- Written consent (DE allows majority consent)

### Approval Thresholds

| Action | Board | Stockholders |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| Stock issuance | Majority | Usually not required |
| Charter amendment | Majority | Majority of outstanding |
| Merger | Majority | Majority of outstanding |
| Sale of all assets | Majority | Majority of outstanding |
| Dissolution | Majority | Majority of outstanding |

## Stockholder Agreements

### Common Agreements at Seed/Series A

**Investors' Rights Agreement (IRA)**
- Information rights
- Registration rights
- Pro rata rights
- Board observer rights

**Voting Agreement**
- Board composition
- Drag-along rights
- Voting covenants

**Right of First Refusal / Co-Sale Agreement (ROFR)**
- Company ROFR on transfers
- Investor co-sale rights
- Exceptions (estate planning, etc.)

### Key Provisions to Review

**Protective provisions (veto rights):**
- Charter amendments
- New stock issuances
- Debt incurrence
- Dividends/distributions
- Change of control
- Executive compensation

**Information rights:**
- Financial statements
- Board meeting attendance
- Inspection rights

## Delaware Franchise Tax

### Annual Requirements

**Due:** March 1 each year
**Filing:** Annual Report

### Calculation Methods

| Method | Basis | Typical Use |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Authorized Shares | # of authorized shares | High valuation startups |
| Assumed Par Value | Assets / issued shares | Lower for most startups |

**Always use assumed par value method** - typically results in minimum tax (~$400-$500)

### Good Standing

**To maintain:**
- Pay franchise tax by March 1
- File annual report
- Maintain registered agent

**If lapsed:**
- Company not in good standing
- Cannot close financing
- May affect contract validity

## Due Diligence Checklist Items

- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation (original and amendments)
- [ ] Bylaws (current)
- [ ] Certificate of Good Standing (recent)
- [ ] All board minutes and consents
- [ ] All stockholder minutes and consents
- [ ] Stock ledger matches charter
- [ ] Franchise taxes current
- [ ] Registered agent current
