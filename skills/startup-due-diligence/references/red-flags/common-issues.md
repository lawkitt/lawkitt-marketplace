## Critical Issues (Deal Blockers)

### Corporate Formation

**No valid incorporation**
- Certificate never filed or rejected
- Entity dissolved for non-payment
- *Risk:* Company doesn't legally exist
- *Fix:* Reinstate or re-incorporate; assess gap period liability

**Unauthorized stock issuances**
- Stock issued without board/stockholder approval
- Exceeds authorized shares in charter
- *Risk:* Stock may be void; securities violations
- *Fix:* Ratify issuances; amend charter; may need stockholder consent

### Cap Table & Securities

**Missing 83(b) elections**
- Founders didn't file 83(b) within 30 days of restricted stock grant
- *Risk:* Massive tax liability when stock vests; cannot fix retroactively
- *Fix:* Cannot cure; assess tax exposure; may affect deal structure

**Options priced below 409A FMV**
- Option exercise price below fair market value at grant
- *Risk:* 409A penalty taxes (20% + interest) on option holders
- *Fix:* Repricing; cash settlements; tax gross-ups

**No 409A valuation for option grants**
- Options granted without independent valuation
- *Risk:* All option grants potentially non-compliant
- *Fix:* Get retroactive 409A (limited cure); may need repricing

### Intellectual Property

**Core IP not assigned to company**
- Founders built product before incorporation, no assignment
- Key contractor/developer didn't assign IP
- *Risk:* Company may not own its product
- *Fix:* Get assignments (may require negotiation/payment); if refused, major problem

**GPL/copyleft code in proprietary product**
- Product contains GPL/AGPL licensed code
- Distributed/deployed without compliance
- *Risk:* May need to open source proprietary code
- *Fix:* Remove/replace code; or open source (usually unacceptable)

### Compliance

**Unlicensed regulated activity**
- Money transmission without license
- Securities broker-dealer without registration
- Healthcare services without compliance
- *Risk:* Regulatory action; fines; business shutdown
- *Fix:* Obtain licenses; restructure business; may be fatal

**Material undisclosed litigation**
- Founders hid pending lawsuits
- Undisclosed regulatory investigation
- *Risk:* Integrity issue; unknown liability
- *Fix:* Full disclosure; assess merits; integrity concern may kill deal

### Founder/Team

**Active founder dispute**
- Founders suing each other
- Unresolved equity ownership disagreement
- *Risk:* Paralysis; cannot close financing; business disruption
- *Fix:* Resolve dispute first; may require mediation; often fatal to deal

## Material Issues (Require Remediation)

### Corporate

**Missing board consents for key actions**
- Stock grants without board approval
- Officer appointments not documented
- Material contracts not approved
- *Remediation:* Ratification consent covering all past actions

**Charter/cap table mismatch**
- More shares issued than authorized
- Stock classes don't match charter
- *Remediation:* Amend charter; reconcile records

**Not in good standing**
- Franchise taxes unpaid
- Annual reports not filed
- *Remediation:* Pay taxes; file reports; reinstate

### Cap Table

**Founder shares not vesting**
- Founders have fully vested shares from day one
- *Remediation:* Founders agree to new vesting schedule as closing condition

**Cap table doesn't reconcile**
- Cap table doesn't match stock ledger
- SAFE/note terms not accurately modeled
- *Remediation:* Full reconciliation; remodel pro forma

**Missing Form D**
- Securities offerings without SEC filing
- *Remediation:* File immediately; assess exposure

### IP

**Missing employee PIIAs**
- Key employees haven't signed IP assignment agreements
- *Remediation:* Get agreements signed immediately (may require consideration)

**Contractor IP not assigned**
- Contractors who worked on product didn't assign IP
- *Remediation:* Obtain assignments; may require payment

**Unknown open source usage**
- No audit of open source in codebase
- License compliance unknown
- *Remediation:* Conduct open source audit; remediate issues

### Employment

**Misclassified contractors**
- People treated as contractors should be employees
- Full-time, exclusive, controlled work
- *Remediation:* Reclassify; pay back taxes/benefits; assess penalties

**No offer letters**
- Employees without written agreements
- *Remediation:* Document retroactively; get signatures

**Key employees without non-competes (where enforceable)**
- Key technical staff could leave and compete
- *Remediation:* Implement appropriate agreements (with consideration)

### Contracts

**Customer concentration >50%**
- Majority of revenue from 1-2 customers
- *Remediation:* Understand contract terms; assess renewal risk; diversification plan

**Material contract terminable on change of control**
- Key customer or vendor can exit on financing
- *Remediation:* Obtain consent or waiver

**Personal guarantees by founders**
- Founders personally liable for company obligations
- *Remediation:* Plan to release post-financing

### Compliance

**Missing state securities filings**
- Blue sky filings not made
- *Remediation:* File in required states

**Privacy policy gaps**
- No privacy policy or non-compliant
- GDPR/CCPA requirements not met
- *Remediation:* Implement compliant policies

## Minor Issues (Clean Up)

### Corporate
- Incomplete meeting minutes
- Missing director resignation letters
- Outdated bylaws

### Cap Table
- Minor cap table discrepancies
- Option paperwork incomplete
- Unsigned grant agreements

### IP
- Domain names in founder's name
- Missing copyright notices
- Trade secret policy not documented

### Employment
- Employee handbook outdated
- Missing I-9 forms
- Training records incomplete

### Contracts
- Missing MSAs for minor vendors
- Auto-renewing contracts not tracked
- Insurance policies need updating

### Compliance
- Labor law postings outdated
- Business licenses need renewal
- Minor tax filings late

## Issue Response Framework

### For Each Issue Identified:

1. **Classify severity** (Critical/Material/Minor)
2. **Describe the issue** clearly and specifically
3. **Explain the risk** (legal, financial, operational)
4. **Propose remediation** with specific steps
5. **Estimate timeline** for resolution
6. **Identify who** needs to act (company, counsel, third party)
7. **Flag if counsel required** for legal matters

### Escalation Triggers

Always escalate to legal counsel when:
- Securities law violations suspected
- Tax positions uncertain
- Litigation risk significant
- Regulatory compliance in question
- IP ownership unclear
- Any Critical issue identified
